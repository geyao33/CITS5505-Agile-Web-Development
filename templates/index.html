Probability

In the nature and society, there are many events will happen certianly. For example, when we throw a stone upward, it must move dowanward at the end. Two like electric charges will repel each other. However, there are some other different events will happen uncertianly, such as, when we toss a coin, we cannot tell it will land on heads or tails. or When we toss a dice, we cannot tell which number it will land on. Therefore, we need probability to describe how likely something is to happen.

Probability of an event = (# of ways it can happen)/(Total number of outcomes)
P(A) = (# of ways A can happen)/(Total number of outcomes)

Example 1
When rolling one dice, there are six outcomes.
1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
What is the probability of rolling a one?
P(1) = 1/6
what is the probability of rolling a one or six?
P(1 or 6) = 2/6 = 1/3

Example 2
When tossing two coins, there are four outcomes.
head and head, head and tail, tail and head, tail and tail.
What is the probability of tossing a head and a tail?
P(head and tail) = 2/4 = 1/2

Statistics

Statistics is the discipline that concerns the collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data. In applying statistics to a scientific, industrial, or social problem, it is conventional to begin with a statistical population or a statistical model to be studied. Statistics deals with every aspect of data, including the planning of data collection in terms of the design of surveys and experiments.
In the statistics, the most important is data. Data are facts and figures that are collected, analyzed, and summarized for presentation and interpretation. Sample survey methods are used to collect data from observational studies, and experimental design methods are used to collect data from experimental studies. The area of descriptive statistics is concerned primarily with methods of presenting and interpreting data using graphs, tables, and numerical summaries.

Expected Value

the expected value of a random variable X, denoted E(X) or E[X], is a generalization of the weighted average, and is intuitively the arithmetic mean of a large number of independent realizations of X.
Expected Value = sum of (probability of an event * outcome)
E(X) = Σ(P(X) * X)

Example 1
If you take 20 questions multiple choice test with A, B, C, D as the answers, and you guess all 'A', what is the expected value?
When we guess answer is 'A', we can expect the probability of guessing right is 25%(0.25). There are 20 question, then the expected value is
E(A) = P(A) * n = 0.25 * 20 = 5

Example 2
When you are rolling a dice, what is the expected value?
The possible outsomes are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6. The probability of each outcome is 1/6. The expected value is
E[X] = 1/6 * 1 + 1/6 * 2 + 1/6 * 3 + 1/6 * 4 + 1/6 * 5 + 1/6 * 6 = 3.5

Rules

Thre are some rules in probability.
1. If an event A certainly will happen, then its probability is 1. P(A) = 1.
2. If an event A certainly will not happen, then its probability is 0. P(A) = 0.
3. complement events: if A denotes the event certainly will happen, then A¯ denotes The event certainly will not happen. P(A) + P(A¯) = 1.
4. If event A belongs to event B, it means when event A happened, event B must happened. It shows A ⊂ B.
If event A belongs to event B, and event B belongs to event A, then event A is same with event B.
5. The set of all elements belongs to A, or to B, or to both. it called the union of A and B. It shows A ∪ B
6. The set of all elements belongs to A and to B, it called the intersection of A and B. It shows A ∩ B.
7. The sum of the probability of each event occurring is equal to one.
8. Addition rule: if A and B represent two mutually exclusive events, then
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
9. Multiplication rule: if A and B represent two independent events, then
P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B)

Conditional Probability

Conditional Probability is the probability of one event occurring with some relationship to one or more other events. The formula of conditional probability.
P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A)

Example
There is a set of poker card except jokers. If you pick one card which is five, what is the probability that this card is heart or club?
Step 1: the probability of pick one card with five is
P(A) = 4/52 = 1/13
Step 2: the probability of pick one card is heart five or club five is
P(A and B) = 2/52 = 1/26
Step 3: Use formula to get answer
P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A) = (1/26) / (1/13) = 1/2

Discrete and Continuous Variables

Discrete Variables are numeric variables that have a countable number of values between any two values. A discrete variable is always numeric.
Continuous Variables are numeric variables that have an infinite number of values between any two values. A continuous variable can be numeric or date/time.

Example
The number of coins, the number of customer, and the number of cups are countable, so they are Discrete Variables.
Time, the length of road, and the weight of rock are not countable, so they are Continuous Variables.
